European indices sold off Monday catching up to Friday's Wall Street rout, compounded by renewed Middle East escalation. Crude near $98 is an inflation input that feeds straight back into move #2 and makes Thursday's CPI print harder to clear.

Counter-Intuitive Take

Consensus reads Broadcom + the jobs-report selloff as "the AI demand story cracking." The better read: demand is fine — it's the supply of paper that should worry you. In one week: the largest IPO in history ($75B), two near-trillion-dollar AI labs filing S-1s, and record AI debt issuance. Insiders and sponsors with the best information in the ecosystem are choosing right now — at these marks, into this tape — to convert private positions into public liquidity. That's not what people do when they think the next leg is up; it's what they do when they think the marks are full.

The six prior DCF theses in this folder all found the same intrinsic-vs-market gap; the issuance wave is the people who set those marks quietly agreeing. The tell to watch isn't Oracle's RPO tonight — it's whether SpaceX's fixed $135 holds above water Friday without a bookbuild's built-in pop. If history's biggest IPO trades flat-to-down just as the rate-cut crutch disappears, the "AI bubble" debate ends not with a crash but with a repricing of every private AI mark on this watchlist — and that's when the build zones in these theses finally come into play.
