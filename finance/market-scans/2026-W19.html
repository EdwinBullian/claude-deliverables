Counter-Intuitive Take

The AMD/PLTR divergence tells us something important about how the AI rally ends.

AMD beat and rallied 20%. Palantir beat harder (EPS +18% vs. AMD's +6%), raised FY growth to +71%, and fell 7%. Hardware traded on visible unit economics; software at premium multiples (PLTR 46x forward) is now trading on the requirement to believe in optionality the DCF can't price. The market has stopped underwriting that optionality at face value.

VC implication: AI software companies that took rounds at 50–100x ARR in late 2025 are about to face mark-down pressure when their next round prices. Watch tier-1 firms' Q3 reporting for first secondary-mark cuts in AI software portcos — that's when the public-private compression becomes visible.
