1. Company Overview

AMD designs CPUs, GPUs, and adaptive-compute silicon. The only segment that matters right now is Data Center, which printed $5.8B in Q1 FY26 (+57% YoY) and is on track for ~$28B full-year FY26. From "cheap CPU alternative to Intel" five years ago to "credible second source for AI accelerators behind NVIDIA" today — one of the cleanest narrative pivots in semiconductors. The Instinct MI300 series got AMD onto Microsoft, Meta, and Oracle hyperscale platforms. OpenAI (6 GW, Oct 2025) and Meta (6 GW, Feb 2026 + 160M-share warrant) together represent ~12 GW of forward GPU capacity — effectively securing the next two years of AI-accelerator growth before MI-series has to compete in the open market.

2. Industry Position & Competitive Dynamics

AI accelerator demand is concentrated in four buyers — MSFT, Meta, Alphabet, Oracle — spending ~$700B combined in 2026. Hyperscalers do not want an NVIDIA monopoly; they are actively underwriting AMD as the second source. This is the structural tailwind. Three threats:

  • Custom silicon (real threat): Google TPU v7, AWS Trainium 3, Microsoft Maia gaining traction on inference. Broadcom (AVGO) fronts every hyperscaler ASIC. If 30–40% of accelerator spend moves in-house by 2028, AMD's TAM expands less than the bull case assumes.
  • NVIDIA's pace: Blackwell B300 ramps 2026; Rubin (R100) late 2026/early 2027. NVDA's CUDA + Mellanox networking + reference rack moat is unchanged. AMD wins on hardware specs in a market that pays for ecosystems.
  • China export controls: Caps the merchant-silicon TAM that AMD competes in. Both NVDA and AMD have engineered down-spec variants.

3. Investment Cases

🟢 Bull Case — $640 (15% weight)

MI400/MI450 hits H2 2026 ramp targets and pulls share from NVDA on training workloads where memory bandwidth dominates (HBM4 advantage). Sovereign AI buildouts (UAE, Saudi, Japan, India) add $15–20B multi-year TAM. Operating margins expand to 35–38% as data center mix exceeds 70% of revenue. Catalysts: Q3 FY26 earnings (data center crosses $8B/quarter), MI450 live H2 2026, MI500 architecture preview.

🟡 Base Case — $280 (50% weight)

AMD hits FY26 revenue $46–47B (in-line with run-rate). FY30 revenue ~$120–130B. OpenAI/Meta contracts deliver as planned, not above. Data center margin tops out at 33–35% non-GAAP. Custom silicon takes 25–35% of AI accelerator TAM by 2028. NVDA Rubin retakes spec leadership late 2027, forcing AMD back into "credible #2" pricing posture.

🔴 Bear Case — $130 (35% weight)

Standard DCF on stated FY26–FY30 assumptions (revenue $34.6B → $106B, op margin 22.5% → 33%) yields $130/share with WACC 14.1% (beta 1.85). This isn't a "story breaks" scenario — it's what conventional valuation says even if every growth assumption hits. A bear story on top: MI450 ramp slips two quarters; NVDA cuts H100/H200 prices; Meta/OpenAI deals reduced. Fair value drops below $100.

4. Valuation Summary

DCF Gap: 5-year DCF on stated assumptions → $133/share at WACC 14.1%, 3.5% TGR. Relaxing WACC to 11% only gets to high-$200s — well short of $455. DCF doesn't justify spot on any reasonable input set.

Comps Premium: AMD at 16.3× EV/CY26 revenue vs. peer median ~14x (defensible on 2× growth). But P/E: AMD at 60.7× CY26 EPS vs. NVDA at 37×. AMD is more expensive than NVIDIA on earnings. Multiple-convergence trade is already priced in.

Reverse DCF: To justify $455, AMD needs ~$160B revenue by FY30 at 30%+ FCF margin — 1.5× the current bull case. Requires AI capex super-cycle to continue at peak intensity for the entire 5-year forecast window.

Probability-weighted FV = 0.35 × $130 + 0.50 × $280 + 0.15 × $640 ≈ $281 vs. spot $455 → -38% expected downside.

5. Key Risks

MI450 H2 2026 ramp slippage — Highest combined P×I. ZT Systems integration is new, HBM4 supply constrained, CoWoS-L packaging shared with NVDA. Any one-quarter delay compresses the FY26 exit run-rate the market is paying for. 25–30% re-rate on the print.
Custom silicon share take (AVGO-fronted) — Structural, slow-moving. Probability rises through 2027–28. Caps AI accelerator TAM at a lower number than the bull case.
NVDA Rubin pricing aggression — NVDA can defend share by pricing H200/B300 down. AMD's quality premium narrative most vulnerable to this.
Multiple compression independent of fundamentals — If AI capex narrative stalls (one Mag-7 cuts capex guide hard), AMD is the highest-beta way to get long AI infra. 30–40% drawdown on market rotation is not a tail event.

Sector Takeaway — What AMD Teaches

Three consecutive theses in (PLTR, CRWD, AMD) and the same pattern keeps showing up: the most narratively powerful AI-infrastructure names trade at prices that no conventional valuation framework can defend. They share: (1) genuinely accelerating fundamentals, (2) hyperscaler customer concentration on the buy side, and (3) limited optionality on the sell side because next-best buyers can build alternatives in-house.

The "second source" trade is structurally more valuable than the market gives it credit for when monopolistic premium pricing is the binding constraint. Hyperscalers will pay AMD a premium just to keep NVDA honest, even if AMD doesn't ship objectively superior silicon. That's worth real money in the EV calculation — but probably not the $500B gap between strict DCF and spot.

Build zone: $300–$350. The historical pattern in semiconductors: dislocations come — usually inside 18 months, usually triggered by inventory correction or one hyperscaler trimming capex guide. Discipline is patience.
