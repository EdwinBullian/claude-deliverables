ANALYTICAL NOTES
• Apple trades at ~34x forward EPS and ~9x EV/FY26E revenue — a premium multiple on ~7-12% revenue growth, the slowest grower in this cohort ex-margin quality.
• On EV/Revenue-to-growth (PEG-style), Apple screens expensive: its multiple is similar to faster growers (Alphabet, Microsoft) and only Nvidia justifies a higher absolute multiple via ~50% growth.
• The 'peer median' here is distorted upward by Nvidia (AI supercycle) and Microsoft/Alphabet cloud — Apple's hardware-led model is structurally lower-growth, so a peer-median multiple flatters Apple.
• Apple's quality (Services mix, ~47% gross margin, ~$99B FCF, buyback shrinking share count ~2-3%/yr) supports a premium — but a premium to a 7% grower is not the same as a premium to a 17%+ grower.
• Net cash is now only ~$44B (vs >$150B historically) as Apple returns ~$100B+/yr — the balance-sheet cushion that once justified a premium has largely been distributed.
• Takeaway: comps say Apple is roughly in line with an already-elevated mega-cap peer set on EV/Rev, but the DCF is the binding constraint — peers being expensive is not a reason Apple is cheap.
