ANALYTICAL NOTES
• Peer set = legacy mass-market OEMs (GM, Stellantis, Toyota, Honda). Tesla excluded: its ~200x fwd P/E and ~15x EV/Rev are a different valuation regime and would distort medians.
• EV multiples use INDUSTRIAL/automotive net debt, not consolidated. Captive finance arms (Ford Credit, GM Financial, Toyota Financial) carry matched debt against receivables; consolidated debt would overstate EV ~3-5x.
• P/E is the cleaner cross-check for captive-finance OEMs. Ford ~11x FY26E sits between cheap GM (~6x) and premium Toyota (~12.5x).
• EV/Rev FY26E peer median ~0.31x implies a Ford price of ~$17 (+22% vs $14.06) — matching the DCF base case.
• EV/EBIT FY26E peer median ~5.0x implies ~$14 — roughly fair vs spot. Comps bracket Ford at ~$14-17.
• Ford screens cheap on FCF yield (~10%) and dividend (~4.3%), but EBIT margin (~5%) trails Toyota (~10%) and GM (~6.5%) — the quality discount is partly justified.
• Bottom line: Ford is modestly cheap-to-fair vs legacy peers; the upside case rests on margin normalization, not multiple re-rating.
