BULL

BASE
BASE ($11): ~23% revenue CAGR to ~$66B FY31E; op margin inflects 5%->25% as Starlink mix rises and xAI losses moderate; capex intensity falls 30%->12% of revenue but stays heavy through the build phase, suppressing near-term FCF (negative through ~2027E). WACC 11.7%, g 3.5%. Strict FCF value ~$11/sh vs $195 ref (-94%) — the DCF is a discipline floor, not a price target, for an infrastructure builder whose value is back-end loaded.
BEAR
BEAR ($1): Starship slips again, capex never normalizes (stays 20%+ of revenue), xAI burn persists, and consolidated FCF stays negative for most of the window. WACC 13.5%, g 2.5%. The near-zero DCF output is itself the lesson: a finite-horizon FCF model ignores the standalone asset value of Starlink (>$100B alone) — which is exactly why SOTP, not DCF, is the right primary tool here.
