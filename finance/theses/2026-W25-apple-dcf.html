SCENARIO NARRATIVES
BULL
BULL ($272, -9%): iPhone 17/18 supercycle sustains, Apple Intelligence drives an upgrade wave, Services compounds mid-teens past a $140B run-rate, and new categories (Vision, home, health) add a growth leg. ~9% revenue CAGR to ~$706B by FY31 at 35% EBIT margins; WACC 8.0%, terminal 3.75%. Even here, intrinsic value sits ~9% below spot.
BASE
BASE ($170, -43%): The most likely path. FY26 +12% on the iPhone 17 cycle, then deceleration to mid-single digits as hardware matures; Services stays the growth/margin engine. ~7% revenue CAGR to ~$623B, EBIT margins ~33%, WACC 9.2%, terminal 3.25%. A best-in-class business — but ~34x forward earnings is far above what the cash flows support.
BEAR
BEAR ($97, -68%): iPhone growth stalls on saturation and China share loss; a Google antitrust remedy pressures the ~$20B+ TAC payment inside Services; AI capex weighs on margins. ~3% revenue CAGR, EBIT margins compress to ~29%, WACC 10.5%, terminal 2.5%. The multiple de-rates toward a GDP-grower's ~18-20x.
