1. Company Overview

Cloudflare operates the world's largest edge network (300+ cities, 120+ countries) — a unified platform combining CDN, cybersecurity (DDoS/WAF/Zero Trust), serverless compute (Workers), storage (R2), and AI-agent infrastructure. Handles ~20%+ of global internet traffic. Land-and-expand SaaS model with 5.5M+ developers on Workers. Q1 2026: $639.8M revenue (+34% YoY), 4,416 $100K+ annual customers (+25% YoY), DBNR 118% (up 7pp YoY), record number of $5M+ customers added. FY2026 guidance: $2.805–2.813B (~30% growth). Revenue reaccelerating from 27% in Q1 2025 to 34% in Q4 2025 — unusual at $2.5B+ ARR scale.

2. Industry Position

Cloudflare sits at the intersection of CDN ($30B+), cybersecurity ($200B+), and edge compute/AI infrastructure (nascent, potentially multi-hundred-billion). No single competitor spans all three. Three structural competitive advantages: network effects (more traffic → better threat intelligence → better security), platform consolidation (118% DBNR quantifies the land-and-expand), and developer ecosystem (5.5M Workers developers create genuine switching costs). Key Q1 event: announced 20% workforce reduction (~1,100 employees), attributing to AI-driven productivity — stock -24% on execution risk concern despite earnings beat.

3. Investment Cases

🟢 Bull Case — $310 (+55%)

Cloudflare is the infrastructure layer for agentic AI — edge network provides lowest-latency routing for AI agents, Workers provides compute, security stack provides trust. If agentic AI adoption follows mobile's 2010–2015 trajectory, NET captures a structural share of every AI-agent interaction. Restructuring is genuinely margin-accretive: ~$200M+ annualized comp savings → non-GAAP op margins from 11% to 24%+ over three years, FCF margins to 20%+ by FY2028. DBNR stabilizes above 120%. DCF bull: $310.

🟡 Base Case — $165 (-17%)

Best-in-class business trading at a premium difficult to justify on traditional metrics. At $200/share, NET trades at ~24x FY2026E revenue and 169x forward earnings — top of the peer range. DCF base (30% initial growth decelerating to 17%, EBIT margins to 24%, 10.5% WACC, 3.0% TGR): implied ~$165. A good outcome — but substantially priced in at $200. Comps: NET trades at significant premium to closest high-growth peers (Datadog 16x, CrowdStrike 17x, Zscaler 8x).

🔴 Bear Case — $75 (-62%)

AI-productivity narrative collapses — if 20% headcount cut was motivated by slowing demand, Q2–Q3 will show it: decelerating revenue, declining DBNR, rising churn. FY26 guide already embeds deceleration to ~28% in H2 from 34% in Q1. If below guide, the 169x forward P/E compresses violently. Hyperscaler commoditization: AWS/Azure/GCP bundling edge compute + CDN + security at cost erodes 74.5% gross margin. Convertible notes ($3.29B) could dilute 15–20%. DCF bear: $75.

Sector Takeaway

Cloudflare is the fourth consecutive thesis (PLTR, CRWD, AMD, NET) revealing the same structural pattern: best-in-class businesses with durable competitive advantages, strong growth, expanding margins — all at valuations traditional DCF says are unjustifiable, yet the market sustains. The consistent gap suggests the market is pricing platform optionality and AI-era TAM expansion that discounted cash flow models, by construction, cannot capture.

Bottom line: Cloudflare is a world-class business at a price that demands near-perfect execution. The post-selloff entry is better than two weeks ago, but base case DCF still implies downside. Build toward $165–175. Q2 earnings July 30 will be the definitive catalyst — the first quarter showing restructuring margin benefits and confirming whether the AI-pivot narrative holds.
