Boring-Software Venture · Research Module 1

Micro-SaaS Niche Shortlist

Evidence-backed niche hypotheses to validate with real humans — not winners, not leads.
Run date: June 10, 2026 · 5 parallel research passes · ~150 sources · spot-check verified
How to read this
Every niche below is a hypothesis, ranked by evidence strength and willingness-to-pay signal — not by how exciting the idea is. The job now is to pick the 2–3 strongest and go find 10 real people with the pain. Judge each one by the quality of its evidence, especially direct "I'd pay for this" quotes, not by the concept.

Top 3 — Start Here

#1HIGH CONFIDENCE ⭐
Restaurant supplier-invoice watchdog
Pain — Keying 10–300 supplier invoices/month into Excel, then manually catching price creep, junk surcharges, short deliveries, and unapplied credits. Vendors rename SKUs to break price tracking.
Who — Independent restaurant owners + bookkeepers (1–5 locations).
"Can I please pay you for this? It sounds awesome." — an owner, replying to a homemade tracking script (the single strongest WTP signal in the run)
Cost of the pain — 50–75 hrs/mo at 200–300 invoices; one catch saved ~$400/mo on a single vendor. On 3–5% net margins, unnoticed 2–3% supplier creep is existential.
Why it's open — MarginEdge/xtraCHEF bundle this into heavy suites at $330–350/location/mo; single-location indies are too small for their sales motion. The real competition is Excel.
Wedge — "Forward invoices to one email; get a weekly text flagging price creep, junk fees, and unapplied credits, with a pre-drafted message to your rep." ~$99/mo.
Surfaced independently in 3 of 5 passes. Hard/defensible core = canonical SKU matching across renamed products.
#2HIGH CONFIDENCE
Restaurant menu sync across delivery platforms
Pain — Updating prices, modifiers, hours, and 86'd items separately on UberEats, DoorDash, Grubhub, the POS, and the website — every change, every platform, by hand.
Who — Independent restaurants & ghost kitchens on 2–4 delivery platforms (same buyer as #1).
"I'm starting to get really tired of dealing with menu updates across Uber Eats, DoorDash, and everything else separately."
WTP — Live Fiverr market of $15–100/change gigs (one literally titled "modify your menus" = repeat work). Restaurants already pay 20–30% commissions, so $79/mo is invisible.
Wedge — "Text your menu change once; live on every platform within the hour, screenshot confirmations included." $79/mo per location.
Risk to validate first: delivery-platform menu-API / portal-automation tolerance — checkable in a weekend with one restaurant's logins.
#3HIGH CONFIDENCE · access-boosted
Greek-life treasurer agent (chasing + continuity, NOT payments)
Pain — A 20-year-old treasurer chasing dues on hated, fee-heavy platforms; books, nationals reports, and IRS 990-N filings falling through the cracks at annual officer turnover.
Who — Chapter treasurers at ~12,000 Greek chapters + adjacent student orgs.
"We use omegafi and it sucks ass… insanely expensive." · "OmegaFi adds a 4–5% accounting fee to every member's bill."
WTP — GreekBill ≈ $3,600/yr in platform fees for a 100-member chapter. Chapters demonstrably pay thousands to incumbents they resent.
Wedge — "Treasurer continuity agent": dues-chasing on top of Stripe + books + nationals reporting + 990-N guardrails that survive turnover. Flat ~$79/mo, no per-member fees.
Your unfair advantage: you land on a Greek-heavy campus in August. Validation cost ≈ ten coffee chats. Stay OUT of payments (Dueflow is racing fees to zero there).
The two-shot plan
Summer (now): restaurants are reachable today and need no special access — validate #1, then #2, with the same walk-in conversations. Fall (August): validate #3 when you're on campus surrounded by the buyer during dues season. Pick one to validate first — #1 has the best evidence and you can test it next week.

Full Shortlist — Ranks 4–12

#4HIGH WTP · MED evidence
Solo-RIA compliance copilot
Pain — Compliance calendar, comms archiving (SEC 204-2), Marketing-Rule review, ADV amendments, exam-binder assembly — all on the founder-advisor personally.
WTP — Compliance consultants run $8–15k/yr; that's the budget to displace. Regulated buyers pay reliably.
Wedge — "Exam-ready in a box" compliance-calendar agent. ~$199/mo.
Best fit with your finance career — but hardest first sale on the list (selling compliance as an 18-year-old is a trust mountain). A "later" play.
#5HIGH CONFIDENCE
HOA violation-letter copilot
Pain — Inspections taking 2–8× longer in clunky software; violation letters auto-sent wrong with no preview; un-customizable collections notices.
"Inspections that used to take me 3 hours are now taking the entire day." · "We cannot create editable notices… terrible for collections."
Wedge — Letter copilot: inspection photo → covenant-cited draft → mandatory human edit → mail-merge + escalation tracking.
Pain concentrated in one nameable role (community manager) you can DM on LinkedIn.
#6HIGH CONFIDENCE
Contractor estimate / scope drafter
"Every week I'm spending 10–20 hours driving out to jobs… then spending my nights building detailed scopes and pricing — all for free."
WTP — Contractor time at $75–150/hr; homeowners already pay $100–200 for detailed estimates.
Wedge — "Record your walkthrough; client-ready scope + bid draft in your pricing format in 20 minutes."
#7MED-HIGH
Gym/studio retention agent (churn watchdog)
"The big thing a lot of software misses is the retention side — following up with no-shows, inactive members, and failed payments."
WTP — Owners pay $100–300/mo for management software; four indie gym-software cos at $25k–100k+ MRR prove the vertical pays.
Wedge — Integration layer on top of existing gym PMSes (not another CRM): "your churn-prevention employee, $99/mo." You lift daily — owner conversations are free.
Risk: API access to incumbent PMSes; crowded-adjacent.
#8MED-HIGH · move fast
MSP QBR/TBR prep assembler
"What takes the most time is data analysis… manual entry, then it gives me a 'shiny PDF'." · "It's all just expensive over-the-top stuff."
Wedge — "Connect your PSA; drafted 3-page TBRs for every client every quarter, in your voice."
Risk: at least two builders already circling the same thread — window closing.
#9MED-HIGH
Reseller cross-listing agent
Pain — Drafting & cross-posting inventory across eBay/Poshmark/Mercari/Depop; existing tools still need a human to write every title/description.
WTP — VAs at ~$0.25–0.50/listing (200/wk) + $30/mo tool subs, simultaneously.
Wedge — "Photo dump to live everywhere" at $0.50/listing, undercutting the VA.
#10MEDIUM
PestPac billing / true-churn overlay
"I've had to create a fake service called monthly billing… we've lost customers because they don't understand why we can't give them one monthly price."
Wedge — "One bill per customer" overlay + true-cancel report on top of PestPac exports.
Caveat: single-source evidence; no community access for you. Validate before falling in love.
#11MEDIUM
Youth-sports treasurer reconciliation
"Powerpay collections go into one main account. This causes a lot of backend reconciliation and money transfers."
Wedge — Auto-reconciliation ledger splitting SportsEngine/TeamSnap payouts by team + weekly treasurer report.
Caveat: volunteer board buyer — slow, seasonal, price-sensitive.
#12MEDIUM
Nonprofit grant-prospect research agent
WTP — $50–60 paid repeatedly on Fiverr for 5-row prospect lists; the sub-$100/mo tier is empty (Instrumentl starts $199+/mo).
Wedge — "5 fresh, deadline-sorted funder matches in your inbox monthly." ~$49/mo.
Caveat: recurrence is per-cycle, not contractual; no community access.
