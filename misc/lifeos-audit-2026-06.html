Life OS · System Audit

Where your Life OS leaks time, energy & attention

A full audit of the Notion workspace, dashboards, widgets, agents & automations — what's broken, what's dormant, what's untracked, and what to upgrade.

Prepared June 6, 2026 · supersedes the May 5 SystemMap audit

00The honest verdict

The architecture is genuinely excellent — that's not the problem. You have 13 scheduled agents, a gamified Notion economy, a deliverables pipeline, an iMessage bridge, and a real coordination layer. Very few people your age (or twice it) run anything this sophisticated.

The problem is the opposite of what you'd expect. You don't need more structure. You have built systems that have quietly decayed or never went live, and a handful of high-leverage life domains with no real tracking at all. The polish on the dashboard is hiding the fact that several of the pipes underneath are dry.

Three things, bluntly: (1) your Health data has been dead for 5+ weeks — the agent is writing reviews on data that doesn't exist. (2) Your Career Hub — the single most important system for your actual IB→VC goal — is an empty shell with zero contacts. (3) Real money is leaking through the cracks right now: a scholarship deadline is 24 days out with nothing started. Fix the dry pipes before adding new ones.

TIER 1Broken / decayed — fix these before building anything new. Garbage in, garbage out.

1.1Health data pipeline is dead — and the agent is faking it

This is the most serious issue in the whole system. Your Health agent runs a full Saturday review and a Sunday plan every week — but it's running on no real data. HRV has been blind for 4+ weeks, Cronometer nutrition logging is sparse-to-empty for 4–5 weeks, and Notion workout logs have been missing for 3 weeks.

The result: the Saturday "weekly review" is theater. It estimates adherence from a step proxy, marks recovery "Moderate" with no HRV, and reports "No data" on nutrition — then the Sunday plan adjusts off that nothing. A health system that can't see the body isn't a health system.

Evidence: pending_actions logs HRV unavailable 4 wks, nutrition missing 4 wks, Notion workouts missing 3 wks; zepp_cli (SyntaxError line 522) and cronometer_cli (database error) both broken; activity log W21 review written retroactively June 4, "HRV blind 4th week… Nutrition No data"; health review offline 5 consecutive weeks.

Fix · high ROI — You now have zepp-health and cronometer MCP connectors wired up. Retire the two broken CLIs entirely and re-point the Health agent at the MCPs. One repair re-lights the entire domain. Until then, the Saturday/Sunday health runs are burning tokens to produce fiction.

Impact: criticalEffort: mediumHealth

1.2Time Log is empty — your weekly review reports numbers that don't exist

The "Time Spent by area" tile in your Sunday review is one of the most valuable things the whole OS could give you: ground truth on where your life actually goes. Except the Time Log database has zero rows. The review either estimates it or skips it. The one quantitative mirror of your attention is hollow.

Evidence: Time Log data source returns no entries; schema has an "Auto/Manual" Source field but nothing is feeding Auto; the database also lives under a separate "Second Brain (1)" parent, detached from the Life OS page.
Fix · near-zero manual effort — Your schedule agent already writes calendar blocks every Sunday. Add a tiny daily job that reconciles calendar events → writes Time Log rows (Source: Auto). You get real time-tracking with no manual logging, and the weekly review stops inventing the number.
Impact: highEffort: lowMeasurement

1.3Research agent fails silently and the queue is chronically jammed

The Tuesday research run died mid-session on June 2 and produced zero deliverables — no folder, no log entry, no signal — and nothing caught it until Thursday's check. Separately, the research queue has sat above its alert threshold for weeks (8–9 items vs. a threshold of 7) with no resolution.

Evidence: pending_actions 2026-06-04 "Research output missing for W23 — run appears to have died mid-session"; queue_alert logged 2026-04-30 and 2026-05-07 (persistent backlog); W23 weekly review grade notes "Research: W23 failure — 0 deliverables."
Fix — Add a same-day retry + hard iMessage alert on any agent that finishes with no deliverable (silent failure is the worst failure). And do a one-time queue triage: trim or promote so it sits at/below threshold.
Impact: highEffort: lowReliability

1.4The Agent Dispatch "Command Center" you built is sitting dark

You shipped the Agent Dispatch system on June 3 — queue DB, Command Center widget, 15-min worker. But the worker is disabled and the Apps Script proxy was never deployed, so the whole thing is built-but-off. Built-and-dark features are worse than no feature: they're cognitive clutter that looks done.

Evidence: scheduled task agent-dispatch-worker shows enabled: false; memory note flags "Eddie must deploy proxy"; Agent Dispatch + AgentOS Settings DBs exist on the Life OS page but the trigger path is inert.
Decide — Either deploy the proxy + enable the worker this week, or consciously shelve it and hide the widgets. Don't leave it half-live.
Impact: mediumEffort: lowAutomation
TIER 2Dormant — built, but unused. Either activate or stop pretending they exist.

2.1Career Hub is a ghost town — your #1 strategic gap

Here's the uncomfortable one. Your stated goal is IB → VC, tech focus. The single highest-leverage activity for that, starting now and especially the summer before ND, is building a network. Your Career Hub has the perfect schema for it — Contacts, Firms, Outreach Log, Coffee Chats, priority tiers, ND grad-year tracking. And it has zero contacts, zero outreach, zero coffee chats.

The page itself still says "Goal: add 10 ND alumni before fall move-in" and the plugin commands that would power it (/network, /alumni-find, /firm-research) are marked "Phase 2 — not built." This is the biggest disconnect between your ambitions and your system. Everything else is polishing how you study and lift; this is the thing that actually moves your career.

Evidence: Contacts data source empty; Career Hub page shows v0.1.0 with all three commands unbuilt; Recruiting Pipeline "empty until ~Spring 2027."
Fix · highest career ROI — Activate it now, not in "Phase 2." A weekly auto-task ("reach out to 2 ND alumni in PE/VC"), Chrome-driven LinkedIn alumni pulls into Contacts, and an auto follow-up task whenever you log outreach. You have a writing plugin with cold-outreach already — wire it to the Hub. Turn the empty schema into a living pipeline before fall.
Impact: criticalEffort: mediumCareer

2.2Finance agent has stopped teaching you

Six consecutive weekly theses have landed on the exact same conclusion: best-in-class platform, DCF-vs-market gap, "avoid at spot." PLTR, CRWD, AMD, NET, DBX, then SpaceX at a –94% gap. The agent itself flagged this as a structural framework gap, not noise. When every output rhymes, you've stopped learning from them — you're just confirming that DCF underprices optionality.

Evidence: pending_actions "finance_sector_pattern_6x"; probability-weighted FV vs spot: PLTR –74%, CRWD –39%, AMD –38%, NET –17%, DBX –39%, SpaceX –94%.
Fix — Diversify the method and the muscle, not just the ticker. Rotate deliverable types: an LBO rep one week, a comps drill the next, an earnings teardown, an optionality/SOTP framework for the hyper-capex names. Force non-tech sectors periodically. The goal is reps across the analyst skill tree, not a 7th "avoid at spot."
Impact: highEffort: lowFinance · Learning

2.3The XP economy's back half may be unused

You built a full RPG layer — XP Ledger, Gold, Loot Table (D20 on Hard tasks), Rewards Shop, Achievements. The front half (earning XP from tasks/habits) clearly drives you. But the back half — actually spending Gold and rolling loot — is where these systems usually die. If you're not redeeming rewards, the economy is one-directional and the dopamine loop is half-open.

Evidence: Rewards Shop, Loot Table, Achievements all exist but tucked in the collapsed Archives toggle; no recent redemption activity surfaced in logs.
Decide — Either make redemption a real ritual (weekly "spend your Gold" in the Sunday review) or simplify. A reward you never claim is just a number.
Impact: mediumEffort: lowMotivation
TIER 3Untracked domains — whole areas of your life with no home in the OS.

3.1You're a finance guy with no personal-finance tracking

The deepest irony in the system. You're building DCFs and studying PE returns, but there is no budget, no spending log, no net-worth or savings tracker for your own money. Investment learning is well covered; personal money management — the thing every adult needs and the thing that compounds longest — has zero presence.

Add — A lightweight Personal Finance DB (income, expenses, savings, simple net-worth) + a monthly review. Even at student scale, the habit is the point, and you'll be glad it exists before your first paycheck.
Impact: highEffort: lowPersonal Finance

3.2No "Summer 2026" or "College Transition" command center

You're in a rare, high-stakes window: high school done (May 13), ND not started. This summer is the last fully free runway you'll have for years, and the move to Notre Dame is a major life event — yet there's no dedicated tracker for either. Course registration, dorm/move logistics, summer goals, orientation, what to lock in before move-in — all of it is floating without a home.

Add — A time-boxed "Summer → ND" project with two lanes: (1) summer accomplishments (finance reps, coding, network-building, fitness bulk), (2) transition logistics with hard dates. This is the most time-sensitive new tracker on the list.
Impact: highEffort: lowLife Transition

3.3Relationships & social life have no presence

Career Hub tracks professional contacts (empty). Nothing tracks the personal side — friends, family, the people you'll want to stay close to when you move cross-country, and the new ones you'll meet at ND. For a major relocation, a light "stay in touch" loop matters more than most productivity tooling.

Add — A minimal relationships tracker (person, last contact, a nudge cadence). It doesn't need to be elaborate — just enough that nobody important goes 3 months without a text by accident.
Impact: mediumEffort: lowSocial

3.4The learning-consumption loop is open

Your Media Curator now fills a Media DB daily and the Resources DB exists — good. But there's no capture of what you actually consumed and what you took from it. Content flows in; nothing closes the loop on "watched / read → one insight → retained." For someone whose edge is learning fast, an open consumption loop leaks the most valuable thing: retention.

Add — A one-tap "consumed + 1 takeaway" field on Media/Resources, optionally surfaced in the weekly review as "what you learned this week." Tiny friction, big compounding.
Impact: mediumEffort: lowLearning
TIER 4Efficiency cuts — kill grunt work, plug leaks, save time & energy.

4.1Deadline leaks — money is falling through right now

This isn't hypothetical. Scholarships 2026 is 24 days from its June 30 deadline with nothing started, and it's been sitting overdue. Your memory has a "never drop overdue tasks" rule, but the system isn't escalating time-sensitive, high-consequence items hard enough — a scholarship is literal free money about to expire.

Fix — A deadline sentinel in the 6 AM daily briefing: scan tasks/scholarships/applications for anything with money or career consequences inside 30 days and escalate loudly (not a quiet reschedule). Overdue ≠ done; high-stakes overdue should get louder each day.
Impact: criticalEffort: lowDeadlines

4.2Database sprawl across two "brains"

Your databases are spread across at least two parent trees — the Life OS page and a separate "Second Brain (1)" page (where Time Log lives). Plus a known unresolved issue: three competing Habit Tracker candidates. Sprawl makes the system harder to trust and to automate against.

Evidence: Time Log parent = "Second Brain (1)"; memory note: "Habit Tracker (3 candidates — needs disambiguation)."
Fix — One consolidation pass: pick the canonical Habit Tracker and archive the other two; pull stray DBs under the Life OS tree (or document why they're separate). Update reference_notion_ids afterward.
Impact: mediumEffort: mediumHygiene

4.3Confirm XP/Gold logging isn't manual grunt work

If you're hand-entering XP/Gold ledger rows when you finish tasks, that's exactly the repetitive work this OS should eat. The point of the gamification is that it's automatic — completing a task should mint its own XP.

Verify — Make sure XP/Gold posts automatically on task completion (formula rollup or an agent step). If it's manual, automate it — never make yourself the data-entry clerk for your own reward system.
Impact: mediumEffort: lowAutomation

4.4Screen-time & sleep behavioral loop has trackers but no teeth

The Screen Time Tracker DB exists but (like Time Log) appears unfed, and sleep data is broken upstream with Zepp. So the "use my phone less / sleep better" loop has the furniture but no signal and no intervention — it can't nudge you because it can't see you.

Fix — Once 1.1 re-lights Zepp, surface a single weekly behavioral nudge ("phone up 22% / sleep down 40 min — pick one to fix"). One actionable line beats a dashboard nobody reads.
Impact: lowEffort: mediumBehavior

05Prioritized roadmap — do them in this order

Sequenced by leverage. The first four are mostly repairs of things you already built — highest return, lowest effort.

  1. Re-light Health via the MCPs. Retire the broken CLIs, point the Health agent at the zepp-health + cronometer connectors. Stops 5 weeks of fictional reviews. (1.1)
  2. Stand up the deadline sentinel and rescue Scholarships 2026 this week — 24 days left. (4.1)
  3. Activate the Career Hub. Weekly "reach 2 alumni" auto-task + LinkedIn pulls + outreach follow-ups. Your highest career-ROI move before fall. (2.1)
  4. Auto-feed Time Log from the calendar. Real attention data with zero manual logging; fixes the weekly-review number. (1.2)
  5. Add silent-failure retry + alerts and triage the research queue. (1.3)
  6. Spin up the Summer → ND transition project. Time-sensitive; the window is now. (3.2)
  7. Diversify the Finance agent across deliverable types and sectors so it teaches again. (2.2)
  8. Add the personal-finance tracker. The habit that compounds longest. (3.1)
  9. Decide on Agent Dispatch — deploy or shelve, don't leave it dark. (1.4)
  10. Consolidation pass — one Habit Tracker, one brain, verify XP auto-posts. (4.2, 4.3)

06The one-line summary

You've out-built your own maintenance. Stop adding; start repairing and activating. Re-light Health, rescue the scholarship, and turn the Career Hub from a beautiful empty schema into the thing that actually gets you to VC. Everything else is polish on a machine whose best parts are currently switched off.
